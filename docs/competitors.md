# Competitor Analysis

## FitnessAI
**Does well:**
Provides guided workout programs that users genuinely enjoy.
Offers workout customization and personalization options.
Attempts to create a complete fitness experience with features like body scans, workout tracking, and progress monitoring.

**Does poorly:**
Inaccurate progress tracking that erodes trust (auto-reduces weights/reps even on successful sessions).
Intrusive share prompts and app advertisements on startup.
Clunky navigation and poor user experience.
Missing cardio logging for activities such as running and treadmill workouts.
Device compatibility issues for body-scan functionality.

**Gap I can fill:**
Real-time, accurate workout data users can trust.
No ads or interruptions during the core fitness experience.
Unified tracking for strength training, cardio, nutrition, and fitness shopping in a single platform.
Consistent feature availability across devices.

## Gym Workout Tracker & Fitness

**Does well:** Provides a clean interface, structured workout plans, personalized recommendations, and motivating progress tracking that helps users stay consistent.

**Does poorly:** Displays intrusive advertisements and promotional content that interrupt the workout experience and make the app feel cluttered.

**Gap I can fill:** Deliver personalized fitness guidance and progress tracking in an ad-free environment where users can focus entirely on their fitness goals.

## Google Fit

**Does well:** Automatically tracks activities, integrates with health data sources, and offers a simple way to monitor daily fitness and wellness metrics.

**Does poorly:** Automatic activity detection can be inaccurate or inconsistent, leading to missed workouts, unreliable records, and limited support for some external fitness devices.

**Gap I can fill:** Provide reliable activity tracking with both automatic detection and manual control, ensuring users always have accurate and trustworthy fitness data.

## Strava

**Does well:** Offers detailed running and cycling analytics, route tracking, performance insights, and a strong fitness community that motivates users.

**Does poorly:** Suffers from GPS inaccuracies, delayed activity tracking, failed activity saves, server issues, and account-access problems that can result in lost or inaccessible workout data.

**Gap I can fill:** Build a reliability-first platform with accurate tracking, secure data storage, and dependable workout recording so users never lose trust in their fitness history.
